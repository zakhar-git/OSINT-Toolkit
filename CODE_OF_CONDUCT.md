## Our standard

We expect precise, respectful, good-faith collaboration. Contributors should challenge claims with evidence, acknowledge uncertainty, accept correction, and protect people whose data appears in research material.

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment, threats, discrimination, or sexualized conduct;
- publishing another person's private information;
- sharing credentials, private communications, or doxxing material;
- promoting unlawful access, stalking, coercion, or deceptive targeting;
- manipulating evidence or presenting inference as established fact;
- sustained disruption of reviews, issues, or community spaces.

## Investigation-specific conduct

Use synthetic examples. Remove unnecessary personal data. Do not name a private person as a target, suspect, or owner in repository discussions. Report tool abuse patterns without reproducing harmful operational detail. A public source is not permission to aggregate or republish everything it contains.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns through a private maintainer channel or GitHub's abuse-reporting tools. Maintainers may edit or remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants. Decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and free from retaliation.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by Contributor Covenant 2.1 and adapted for an open-source intelligence documentation community. The enforcement address is repository-specific and must be configured by the adopting maintainers before public launch.
